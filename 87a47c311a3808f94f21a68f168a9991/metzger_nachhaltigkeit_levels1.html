🌱 Butcher Sustainability Trainer

Choose your English level to start practicing sustainable decision-making in the butcher's trade.

🟢 A1–A2 (Beginner Level)

Goal: Write simple but meaningful bullet points about sustainable actions. Short notes or sentence starters are enough.

💬 Useful Sentence Starters

  • Use less … (plastic / water / energy)
  • Buy local … (meat / products / food)
  • Don’t waste … (food / paper / meat)
  • Reuse … (boxes / bags)
  • Clean with … (eco-friendly soap / little water)
  • Talk to … (customers / boss) about … (recycling / saving energy)
  • Check … (temperature / machines)

🧠 Tip: Use 2–3 notes per task. Example: “use paper instead of plastic”, “buy meat from local farm”, “save energy in the cooling room”.

🟡 B1 (Intermediate Level)

Goal: Write reasoned action points using short sentences and simple connectors like because, so, to reduce, instead of.

💬 Sentence Frames

  • Reduce ___ because it wastes energy.
  • Use ___ instead of ___.
  • Buy from ___ to support local farms.
  • Plan ___ better to avoid waste.
  • Tell customers about ___.
  • Check if ___ can be recycled.
  • Choose ___ to save resources.

🔑 Connectors / Linking Words

because • to reduce • instead of • as well as • in order to

📘 Example Notes

  • buy from local supplier to reduce CO₂
  • use less plastic packaging
  • plan orders better → less waste

🔵 B2–C1 (Advanced Learners)

Goal: Write clear, well-reasoned actions and justifications using professional vocabulary and complex structures.

💬 Sentence Patterns

  • Implement ___ to minimize environmental impact.
  • Raise awareness among ___ about ___.
  • Replace ___ with a more sustainable alternative.
  • Optimize ___ to reduce waste / emissions / costs.
  • Ensure that ___ is sourced locally and ethically.
  • Review ___ regularly to maintain efficiency.

🧠 Functional Vocabulary

minimize / maximize • efficiency / resource use / supply chain • reduce footprint / raise awareness • sustainable sourcing • energy-efficient • food waste management

📘 Example Notes

  • replace plastic trays → compostable materials
  • educate staff on waste sorting
  • monitor energy use in cooling systems

💡 Focus on impact, process, responsibility. Example: “optimize cutting plans to reduce leftover meat”.

🔴 Native Speakers / Highly Proficient Learners

Goal: Formulate precise, professional, and reflective sustainability actions with nuance and style.

💬 Style Patterns

  • Introduce a system to track and reduce waste.
  • Encourage customers to bring reusable containers.
  • Align sourcing strategy with ethical suppliers.
  • Invest in energy-efficient refrigeration units.
  • Foster a culture of sustainability among staff.

🧠 Sophisticated Vocabulary

to prioritize / to implement / to align with • environmentally conscious / ethically sourced / resource-efficient • traceability / transparency / life-cycle approach • zero-waste policy / sustainable mindset

📘 Example Notes

  • integrate traceability standards in the supply chain
  • replace single-use packaging with biodegradable options
  • conduct internal sustainability audits

💡 Tip for sounding “sophisticated”: Combine verbs + abstract nouns: promote sustainability, reduce impact, enhance efficiency. Avoid simple verbs like do or use — say implement, introduce, adopt, prioritize.
